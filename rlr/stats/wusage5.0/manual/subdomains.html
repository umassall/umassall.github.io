Please Note: this option has been largely replaced by the much simpler subdomainsof option. We recommend that you consider using that option instead. The subdomains option, which extends for several lines and must end with the line end subdomains, is used to grant certain subdomains the same importance normally accorded to a top-level domain. If a subdomain is listed in the subdomains option, it will appear in the ranking of domains and the domain pie charts alongside other top-level domains. This option is useful for company Intranets, on which most accesses come from subdomains within the company and not from other top-level domains. Listing a subdomain in the subdomains option does not guarantee that, for instance, it will be one of the top ten domains accessing the server most often. If you wish to examine small subdomains or individual machine host names on a server that is accessed by the outside world, you may wish to use the allowsites option to exclude outside accesses from your analysis. If the subdomains ... end subdomains option and the subdomainsof option are both absent, only top-level domains will appear in the domains listing and pie chart.
