Adaptive Agent Resources

The purpose of this web site is to provide resources for designing and building adaptive agents.

Agents are programs that perform a goal on behalf of a user.

Adaptive agents use machine learning techniques to achieve their goal.

Standards under development for Multi-Agent Interactions

Knowledge Interchange Format (KIF) - is a common language for expressing the content of a knowledge base, and is being developed by the Interlingua Group of the Knowledge Sharing Effort (KSE). IBM's ABE (see Agent Building Environments) suppports KIF.

Shared Reusable Knowledge Bases (SRKB) addresses the issue of facilitating consensus on the content of sharable knowledge bases, and is being developed by the Shared Reusable Knowledge Bases group of the KSE.

Knowledge Query and Manipulation Language (KQML) was developed to address communication between two knowledge-bases systems, or between a knowledge-based system and a conventional database management system, and was developed by the External Interfaces Group of the KSE.
